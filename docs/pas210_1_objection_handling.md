# Objection Handling Guide

**Principle:** Every objection is a **diagnostic opening**, not a wall. Don't argue — agree, then ask a question that reveals whether the thing they're confident about is actually true. Let *their own answer* create the doubt. No hype, no pressure, no AI evangelism.

**The move (use it everywhere):** *Acknowledge → Ask → Reframe to visibility/continuity.*

---

## "We already have a CRM."
**Acknowledge:** "Good — you should. PAS isn't a CRM and won't replace it."
**Ask:** "When a lead slips — a missed call, a forgotten callback — does your CRM *tell* you, or do you have to go digging?"
**Reframe:** "Right. A CRM is a filing cabinet — it stores what already happened. PAS is the set of eyes that answers the phone when no one can, and tells you what's slipping *right now.* It sits on top of your CRM, it doesn't compete with it."

---

## "We already respond quickly."
**Acknowledge:** "That's genuinely a strength — most don't."
**Ask:** "During business hours, sure. What happens to a lead that calls at 8pm Saturday? And what % of calls, honestly, hit voicemail when your agents are showing property?"
**Reframe:** "Even great teams can't answer every call — they're with clients. That's not a people problem, it's a coverage problem. PAS catches exactly the calls your fast team physically can't, so 'quick' becomes 'every time.'"

---

## "My agents handle this."
**Acknowledge:** "And they should own the relationship — PAS doesn't change that."
**Ask:** "When an agent's in a showing and three leads call, who handles those? And if one forgets a callback, would you ever know?"
**Reframe:** "PAS doesn't replace your agents — it covers the gaps they can't, and it gives *you* the visibility to know nothing fell through. It makes your good agents look even better because they only get clean, qualified, reachable leads."

---

## "We don't need AI."
**Acknowledge:** "Honestly, agreed — don't buy AI."
**Ask:** "Can I ask it differently: do you ever lose leads you paid for because no one reached them in time, or a callback got missed?"
**Reframe:** "That's the only question that matters. Buy the *outcome* — every call answered, nothing slipping, full visibility — and judge it on whether fewer leads leak. The technology under the hood is irrelevant if the leakage stops."

> Never defend the AI. Redirect to leads and money every time.

---

## "We aren't losing leads."
**Acknowledge:** "That'd be great — and possible."
**Ask:** "Let's confirm it: could you tell me right now how many leads you didn't reach yesterday, and how many callbacks were promised this week and actually made?"
**Reframe:** "If you can't see it, you can't know you're not losing it — and silent leakage is the most expensive kind. That's exactly what the audit settles. If you're truly tight, I'll tell you so and we both move on. Worst case you get a free diagnostic."

> This is your strongest play: turn confidence into a 20-minute audit. The numbers do the persuading.

---

## "We're too small."
**Acknowledge:** "Small can be the right time, actually."
**Ask:** "With a lean team, what's the cost of a single lead you paid for and never reached?"
**Reframe:** "Smaller shops feel every lost lead *more* — there's no volume to hide it. PAS gives you enterprise-grade coverage and visibility without hiring a front desk or a sales ops person. And as a design partner, founder pricing is built for your size."

---

## "We're too busy."
**Acknowledge:** "That's precisely the symptom."
**Ask:** "How much of 'too busy' is chasing your team for status and handling leads that were never qualified?"
**Reframe:** "PAS exists to *reduce* that load — it answers and qualifies so your team only touches real leads, and it gives you one place to see what needs attention instead of chasing updates. Setup is light: point your number at it, connect your calendar. The demo takes five minutes."

---

## Universal fallbacks

- **Stall / "send me info":** "Happy to — but a one-pager won't tell you if *you* have leakage. Give me 20 minutes for the audit and you'll have your own numbers. Worth it?"
- **Price-first:** "Let's make sure there's something worth pricing — if the audit shows you're not losing leads, there's nothing to sell. If it shows you are, the number will be small next to the leakage."
- **Risk fear:** "PAS is read-only — it can't change or delete anything in your pipeline, and it doesn't act on its own. It only answers calls and shows you the truth. The downside is close to zero."
- **"Does it work?":** "Let's not take my word — we'll run it on your real inbound number during the design-partner phase and measure your own continuity numbers."

---

## What NOT to do
- ❌ Don't argue or get defensive.
- ❌ Don't pitch features or roadmap.
- ❌ Don't evangelize AI.
- ❌ Don't promise outbound automation or anything PAS doesn't do today.
- ✅ Do convert confidence into the [Lead Continuity Audit](pas210_1_lead_continuity_audit.md) — every time.
