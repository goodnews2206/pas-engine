# Discovery Call Framework

**Goal:** Determine whether this brokerage is actually experiencing **lead continuity failures** — and whether they're a fit for the design-partner program. Discovery is qualification, not pitching. If they're not leaking leads, find that out fast and move on with your reputation intact.

**Rules:**
- Ask open questions. Shut up after each one.
- Quantify everything you can ("roughly how many," "what percent," "how often").
- Never lead with AI. Lead with leads, time, money, and visibility.
- Take notes verbatim — their words become your demo and your proposal.

---

## 1. Opening questions (rapport + frame)
- "Tell me how leads come into your brokerage today — walk me through a typical one."
- "What's working well in how you handle leads right now?" *(Earn trust by acknowledging strengths first.)*
- "If you could wave a wand and fix one thing about your lead flow, what would it be?"

---

## 2. Qualification questions (is there a real business here?)
- How many agents do you have actively working leads?
- Roughly how many new leads per week, across all sources?
- What's a closed deal worth to you on average in commission?
- Are you growing, holding, or trying to do more with the same team?
- Who decides on tools like this — just you, or others too?

> If lead volume is tiny and there's no budget authority, qualify gently toward "too small for now, let's stay in touch."

---

## 3. Operational visibility questions (the core wedge)
- Right now, can you see today's lead activity across all your agents in one place?
- If I asked how many leads you didn't reach yesterday, could you answer?
- When a lead slips through the cracks, how do you find out — and how fast?
- Where does the truth about your pipeline live: a CRM, spreadsheets, or people's heads?
- How much of your week goes to chasing your team for status updates?

---

## 4. Lead handling questions (first contact)
- When a lead calls during the day, who picks up? What about evenings and weekends?
- What percent of inbound lead calls would you guess get answered live vs. voicemail?
- How fast, realistically, does a new lead get a first human response?
- What happens to a lead that comes in at 9pm on a Friday?

---

## 5. Follow-up questions (continuity over time)
- When a lead asks for a callback at a specific time, where does that live and who owns it?
- Could you produce a list of every promised callback this week and whether it happened?
- What's your follow-up cadence for a qualified-but-not-yet-ready lead — and is it enforced?
- When an agent forgets to follow up, does anyone notice?

---

## 6. Current-tooling questions (fit + integration reality)
- What CRM or tools do you use today? What do they do well / badly?
- Does your current stack tell you what's *slipping*, or just store records?
- Have you tried anything to solve missed calls or missed follow-ups before? What happened?
- What would have to be true for a new tool to be worth the hassle of adopting it?

> Listen for "our CRM stores everything but tells us nothing" — that's your exact opening (PAS is the *intelligence* layer, not another CRM).

---

## 7. Design-partner fit questions
- Are you open to working closely with an early-stage team and shaping the product?
- Can you commit ~30 minutes of feedback every couple of weeks?
- Are you comfortable being an early reference if this works well for you?
- Is someone on your team willing to actually use a new operational view day to day?

---

## Scoring the call (fit signal)

Mark each: ✅ strong / ➖ neutral / ❌ poor.

| Signal | What good looks like |
|---|---|
| Real lead volume | Enough leads that slippage = real money |
| Felt pain | They *volunteer* frustration about missed leads/visibility |
| Visibility gap | Can't answer "what slipped yesterday?" |
| Budget/authority | Can say yes (or strongly influence it) |
| Collaboration appetite | Genuinely up for a design-partner relationship |

- **4–5 ✅** → move to/confirm the [Lead Continuity Audit](pas210_1_lead_continuity_audit.md) and pitch design partnership.
- **2–3 ✅** → nurture; run the audit to crystallize pain.
- **0–1 ✅** → disqualify warmly; leave the door open.
