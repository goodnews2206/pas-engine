# Lead Continuity Audit Framework

**Use live, on a call.** ~20 minutes. The goal is for the **broker to count their own leakage out loud.** You facilitate; you do not pitch. By the end they should see, in their own numbers, where leads slip and what it costs.

**Doctrine link:** PAS makes the company *queryable, adaptive, and operationally intelligent.* This audit demonstrates the queryability gap before PAS, so the value is obvious.

---

## 1. Audit purpose

To answer one question with evidence: **"Of the leads you already pay for, how many slip away unseen — and what is that worth?"**

Three outcomes, any of which is a win:
1. The broker discovers real, quantified leakage → strong PAS fit.
2. The broker discovers they *can't even measure it* → visibility is the wedge.
3. The broker genuinely has it handled → you disqualify fast and keep your credibility.

Position it as **free and useful regardless of outcome.** You are giving them a diagnostic, not a sales trap.

---

## 2. Brokerage discovery questions (set the frame)

- How many agents are actively taking leads right now?
- Roughly how many new leads come in per week across all sources?
- Who answers a lead call when it comes in — a specific person, a rotation, or whoever's free?
- What happens to a lead that calls at 7pm on a Saturday?
- If I asked you *right now* how many leads you didn't reach yesterday, could you tell me?

> The last question is the hook. Most can't answer it. That's the audit thesis in one beat.

---

## 3. Lead source analysis

Map where leads enter and how "warm" each source is.

| Source | Volume/wk | Cost | First-touch owner | Tracked? |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Portals (Zillow/Realtor/etc.) | | | | |
| Website forms | | | | |
| Sign / phone calls | | | | |
| Referrals | | | | |
| Social / paid ads | | | | |

Diagnostic questions:
- Which source do you pay the most for? Which converts best? Do you actually know, or is it a guess?
- When a portal lead comes in, how does it physically reach a human — text, email, app ping, CRM?
- **Leak probe:** between the source and a human picking up the phone, how many handoffs are there? (Every handoff is a drop point.)

---

## 4. Response-time analysis

The single highest-leverage number in real estate lead conversion.

- When a lead comes in during business hours, how long until someone actually speaks to them — minutes, hours?
- After hours? Weekends?
- What % of inbound lead **calls** get answered live vs. go to voicemail? (Push for a real estimate.)
- Of voicemails, how many get a callback the same day?

**Reflect the cost:**
> "Industry pattern is that the first brokerage to reach a lead wins them most of the time, and reachability collapses after the first few minutes. If even [their estimate]% of your calls go to voicemail, that's [X] paid leads a week effectively handed to whoever calls back first."

---

## 5. Callback analysis

Where promised continuity quietly dies.

- When a lead asks to be called back at a specific time, where is that captured? (A sticky note? An agent's memory? Nowhere?)
- Who owns making sure that callback happens?
- If an agent forgets, does anyone notice? Is there a list of missed callbacks anywhere?
- **Leak probe:** could you produce, right now, a list of every callback promised this week and whether it happened?

> A "no" here is gold. Callbacks are the most visible broken promise to a buyer.

---

## 6. Follow-up analysis

- After the first contact, what's the follow-up cadence — and is it enforced or "hopefully"?
- What happens to a qualified-but-not-ready lead? Does it get nurtured or forgotten?
- How do you know an agent followed up vs. just said they did?
- When an agent leaves, what happens to their pipeline?

---

## 7. Visibility analysis

The PAS wedge. Most brokerages are operating blind.

- Can you see, in one place, today's lead activity across all agents?
- Can you ask "what needs attention right now?" and get an answer in seconds?
- Do you know which high-value leads are currently unassigned or waiting?
- When something slips, do you find out the same day, the next week, or never?
- Where does this information live — CRM, spreadsheets, agents' heads?

**Reflect:**
> "So most of what we've discussed — missed calls, missed callbacks, unassigned high-value leads — you currently can't see in one place, in real time. That's exactly the gap PAS closes: it makes your pipeline *queryable.*"

---

## 8. Risk scoring framework

Score each dimension 0–3 (0 = handled, 3 = blind/leaking). Total /18.

| Dimension | 0 (Strong) | 1 | 2 | 3 (At risk) | Score |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| After-hours coverage | 24/7 live answer | Most evenings | Sporadic | Voicemail only | |
| First-response time | < 5 min always | < 30 min | Hours | Often next day | |
| Call answer rate | ~all answered | Most | About half | Many to voicemail | |
| Callback reliability | Tracked + enforced | Tracked | Ad hoc | Untracked | |
| Follow-up enforcement | Systematic | Mostly | Inconsistent | None | |
| Operational visibility | Real-time, one place | Daily-ish | Manual digging | Effectively blind | |

**Interpretation:**
- **0–5 — Tight ship.** Disqualify honestly or position PAS as visibility insurance.
- **6–11 — Leaking quietly.** Strong fit. Focus on the 2–3 highest-scoring dimensions.
- **12–18 — Bleeding.** Urgent fit. Lead with the single most painful finding.

**Estimate the cost (do the math with them):**
> Weekly leads × estimated slip-rate × close rate × average commission = monthly revenue at risk.
> Even a conservative version of this number is usually larger than any plausible PAS price — say it out loud and let it sit.

---

## 9. Closing the audit

> "Here's what we found together: [score] out of 18, with the sharpest gaps in [top 2 dimensions], and a conservative estimate of [$X/month] in leads slipping unseen. None of this required AI to diagnose — it's just visibility you don't have today. PAS is how you close those specific gaps: every call answered, every callback captured, and a live, plain-English view of what needs attention. Want to talk about doing this together as a design partner?"
