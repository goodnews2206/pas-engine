# ORVN — Content Strategy

## Objective

Content exists to make one idea inevitable in the mind of a brokerage operator:

> **Your brokerage's biggest losses are operational, not marketing. PAS is the infrastructure that fixes that — and gives your brokerage a memory.**

Content is not for "awareness" in the abstract. It is for moving a specific person (the ICP) from *"I have a people/effort problem"* to *"I have a structural/infrastructure problem, and there's a layer that solves it."*

## ICP (who the content is for)

**US real estate broker owners, team leads, and sales managers.** Decision-makers and operators who feel the operational pain daily and have budget or influence over fixing it. (Full detail in `ICP_AND_MARKET_CONTEXT.md`.)

## Core topics (the content pillars)

Every piece should ladder up to at least one of these:

1. **Speed-to-lead** — the minutes-matter reality; how fast a lead is contacted decides whether it converts.
2. **Lead leakage** — leads that arrive and quietly die; the money lost in the cracks.
3. **ISA failure** — why Inside Sales Agents underperform, churn, and don't scale; why the function keeps breaking.
4. **Inconsistent follow-up** — follow-up that depends on whether a human remembered, and therefore doesn't happen.
5. **Agent accountability** — who actually did what; closing the gap between "assigned" and "done."
6. **Operational visibility** — being able to *see* the operation, not guess at it.
7. **Brokerage memory** — knowledge that lives in people's heads and walks out when they leave.
8. **PAS Brain** — the durable institutional memory layer; the headline product idea.
9. **Infrastructure vs tools** — the reframe: brokerages keep buying tools to fix a problem that needs infrastructure.

## Narrative spine (how a piece is built)

Daniel's content follows a recognizable structure (see `DANIEL_VOICE_AND_TONE.md`):

> **anecdote → structural insight → reframe → operator question**

1. **Anecdote** — a concrete, real moment from the field (a lead lost, an ISA quitting, a follow-up that never happened).
2. **Structural insight** — name the *system* failure underneath the anecdote, not the person.
3. **Reframe** — shift from "we need to try harder / hire better" to "this is an infrastructure problem."
4. **Operator question** — end with a question that makes the reader audit their own operation.

## Positioning rules

- **Infrastructure, not tools.** Always pull toward "the layer you run on," away from "another app."
- **Not a CRM / chatbot / social app.** Pre-empt the miscategorization.
- **Structural, not motivational.** We don't tell operators to hustle harder; we show them the system is the problem.
- **Memory is the moat.** When in doubt, bring it back to the PAS Brain and brokerage memory.

## What good content does

- Names a pain the operator already feels but hasn't articulated.
- Reframes it from a people problem to a structural one.
- Leaves the reader slightly uncomfortable about their own operation (in a productive way).
- Never hypes, never over-polishes, never sounds like a press release.
