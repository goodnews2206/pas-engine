# PAS Founder Demo Script

**Purpose:** Let a brokerage owner understand PAS in under 5 minutes, and let the founder run every demo the same way, every time.

**Doctrine:** *PAS makes your company queryable, adaptive, and operationally intelligent.*

**Tone rules:**
- Lead with **lost leads and lost revenue**, not with AI.
- You are a **diagnostician**, not a vendor. Ask, observe, reflect back.
- PAS is **read-only** today. Never promise it dials, texts, or changes their data. It *answers the phone, qualifies, books, and shows them the truth about their pipeline.*
- Silence is a tool. Ask a question, then stop talking.

---

## 0. Before you start (10 seconds)
Confirm you know: their brokerage name, roughly how many agents, and where their leads come from (portals, website, referrals). If you don't, that's your first discovery question — not a demo blocker.

---

## 1. The 30-second opening

> "Thanks for the time. I'll keep this short. I work with brokerages on one specific problem: **the leads you already paid for that quietly slip away** — the call that goes to voicemail, the callback nobody made, the form that sat for a day. Most brokerages can't see it happening, so it never gets fixed. I'd like to ask you a few questions about how leads move through your shop, and then show you how we make that completely visible. Fair?"

Then **stop**. Let them respond. You've framed the problem as theirs, not as a product.

---

## 2. The 60-second problem framing

Deliver this as a story, not a pitch:

> "Here's the pattern we see in almost every brokerage. A lead comes in — Zillow, your website, a sign call. The first brokerage to reach them usually wins them. But your agents are showing property, in the car, with another client. So the call rings out. The lead fills another form on another brokerage's site. By the time someone calls back — if anyone does — they've moved on.
>
> The painful part isn't that it happens. It's that **nobody sees it happen.** There's no list at the end of the day that says: *here are the seven leads we never reached, here are the three callbacks we promised and missed, here's the one high-value buyer still sitting unassigned.* The lead just disappears, and the cost disappears with it.
>
> What I do is make that invisible leakage visible — and make sure the first contact actually happens, every time, day or night."

Key phrases to land: **"leads you already paid for," "first to reach them wins," "nobody sees it happen," "the cost disappears with it."**

---

## 3. The 5-minute demo walkthrough

Run these five beats in order. Each is ~1 minute. Narrate what the broker *cares about* (the lead, the booking, the visibility), not the technology.

### Beat 1 — First contact that never drops (≈60s)
- Show a call coming into PAS (live number or the recorded/simulated demo call).
- Narrate: *"A lead calls. PAS answers on the first ring — 24/7. It talks like a person: asks what they want to do, what they can spend, and when they're looking to move."*
- Land it: *"This is the call that would have gone to voicemail. It didn't."*

### Beat 2 — Qualification in their words (≈60s)
- Show the structured result: intent (buy/sell/rent), budget, timeline captured.
- Narrate: *"Every answer is captured in the lead's own words and saved as a clean record. Your agent doesn't start from zero — they start from a qualified lead."*

### Beat 3 — Booking or callback, nothing lost (≈60s)
- Show the booking branch (calendar) **or** the callback branch.
- Narrate: *"If they're ready, PAS books the viewing on your calendar. If they're not, it captures the callback — the time they want and the best number — and confirms it before hanging up. Either way, the lead doesn't fall through."*

### Beat 4 — The end-of-day truth (≈60s) — *the emotional peak*
- Show the operational view / observer digest (the "what needs attention" surface).
- Narrate: *"Here's the part most owners have never had. At any moment you can ask: what needs attention? And PAS tells you — leads waiting, callbacks overdue, high-value buyers unassigned, calls that failed. This is your pipeline, queryable, in plain English."*
- If you have a live tenant with the snapshot bridge enabled, show **their** data and say the source out loud: *"This is live — your numbers, not a sample."*

### Beat 5 — Ask it like a person (≈60s)
- Show the Slack surface: type `what needs attention` / `calls today` / `hot leads`.
- Narrate: *"Your team already lives in Slack or their phone. They just ask PAS, in plain language, and it answers — read-only. PAS watches and reports. It never touches your CRM, never calls anyone on its own, never changes your data. It's an honest set of eyes on your operation."*

**Close the demo with one sentence:**
> "So: every call answered, every lead qualified, nothing lost between the call and the calendar — and for the first time, you can *see* it."

---

## 4. Common broker questions (fast answers)

| They ask | You say |
|---|---|
| "Does it replace my agents?" | "No. It makes sure your agents only spend time on real, qualified, reachable leads. It does the part they physically can't — answer every call instantly." |
| "Does it call leads for me?" | "Today PAS is observe-and-answer: it answers inbound, qualifies, books, and shows you what's slipping. It does not auto-dial or text on its own. That's deliberate — we earn trust before we touch outbound." |
| "Where does the data live?" | "Your leads and calls are stored per-brokerage, isolated from every other client. PAS reads your data to answer your questions; it doesn't expose it elsewhere." |
| "What does it cost?" | "Design partners get founder pricing in exchange for working closely with us. Let's first confirm you actually have leakage worth fixing — that's the audit." |
| "How long to set up?" | "Point your inbound number at PAS and connect your calendar. We can demo on your real number within days." |
| "Is this an AI thing?" | "It uses good technology under the hood, but what you're buying is *lead continuity and visibility.* Judge it on whether fewer leads slip, not on the buzzword." |

---

## 5. Transition into the Lead Continuity Audit

When the broker is nodding — or pushing back with "we don't really lose leads" — pivot to diagnosis:

> "Let's not guess. I run a short **Lead Continuity Audit** — about 20 minutes — where we walk through where your leads come from, how fast you reach them, what happens to callbacks, and what you can actually see today. At the end you'll have a clear picture of where leads leak and what it's costing you, whether or not you ever use PAS. Want to do that now, or set a time this week?"

## 6. Transition into the design-partner discussion

After the audit surfaces real leakage:

> "Based on what we just walked through, you've got [specific finding — e.g. 'no visibility on after-hours calls and no list of missed callbacks']. That's exactly what we fix. I'm taking on a small number of **design partners** — brokerages we build alongside. You get founder pricing and direct influence on the product; we get a real operation to learn from. It's collaborative, not a typical vendor relationship. Can I walk you through how that works?"

---

## Founder reminders
- **Never lead with AI.** Lead with the lost lead.
- **Don't oversell the roadmap.** Sell what PAS does today: answer, qualify, book, reveal.
- **Let the audit do the closing.** When *they* count their own leakage, the product sells itself.
- **Read-only is a feature.** "PAS never changes your data or acts on its own" lowers the risk of saying yes.
