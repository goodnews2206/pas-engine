# PAS Design Partner Program

**What it is:** A small, hand-picked group of brokerages we **build PAS alongside** — not customers we sell to, partners we develop with. They get founder pricing and direct product influence; we get a real operation to learn from and prove value in.

**Why it exists:** PAS is early and deliberately read-only. The fastest path to a product brokerages can't live without is to embed with a few real operations and solve their actual lead-continuity problems in the open.

**Doctrine:** PAS makes a company *queryable, adaptive, and operationally intelligent.* Design partners are how "adaptive" is earned — the product adapts to real brokerages before it adapts anyone's behavior.

---

## 1. Program objectives

- **For the partner:** stop losing leads they already pay for, and gain real-time operational visibility they don't have today.
- **For PAS:** validate the doctrine against real pipelines, calibrate the observer against real data, and produce a referenceable success story.
- **Shared:** prove, with the partner's own numbers, that fewer leads slip after PAS than before.

---

## 2. What the partner gets

- **Founder pricing** locked for the partnership term (and protected after).
- **Direct product influence** — their workflows shape the roadmap.
- **White-glove setup** — we point their inbound number at PAS and connect their calendar with them.
- **A live operational view** — "what needs attention," calls today, callbacks due, unassigned high-value leads — on **their** data once the live snapshot is enabled.
- **Direct line to the founder.** No support queue.

---

## 3. Expectations (what we ask in return)

- **Real usage.** At least one person uses the operational view as part of their routine.
- **Honest feedback.** Tell us what's confusing, missing, or wrong.
- **Access to ground truth.** Willingness to let us observe (read-only) their real lead/call/booking data so the product can be calibrated to their operation.
- **A reference, if it works.** If PAS delivers, they're willing to be named/quoted to future partners.
- **Reasonable patience.** Early product; some rough edges. We move fast on what matters to them.

---

## 4. Feedback cadence

| Touchpoint | Frequency | Format | Purpose |
|---|---|---|---|
| Async check-in | Weekly | Slack/text | Quick "what's working / what's annoying" |
| Working session | Every 2 weeks | 30 min call | Review findings, prioritize next changes |
| Operational review | Monthly | 45 min | Did fewer leads slip? Look at the numbers together |
| Founder pulse | Ad hoc | Direct | Anything urgent, anytime |

Keep it lightweight. The bar is *consistent and honest*, not heavy.

---

## 5. Implementation boundaries (read this verbatim to the partner)

PAS today is **observe-and-answer**. During the design-partner phase:

- ✅ PAS **answers** inbound calls, qualifies leads, books viewings, captures callbacks, and **shows** operational status.
- ✅ PAS reads the partner's data **per-brokerage, isolated** from every other client, to answer their questions.
- ❌ PAS does **not** auto-dial or text leads on its own.
- ❌ PAS does **not** modify, delete, or write into the partner's CRM or lead data.
- ❌ PAS does **not** take autonomous actions on the partner's behalf.
- Any future capability that crosses these lines is **opt-in, discussed first, and never silent.**

> This boundary is a selling point: the partner gets value with near-zero risk because PAS can't break their pipeline — it can only watch it and answer the phone.

---

## 6. Success metrics

Agree on these **before** go-live so success is provable, not anecdotal.

**Primary (continuity):**
- Inbound lead calls answered live (target: near 100%, including after hours).
- Promised callbacks captured and visible (target: 100% captured).
- Reduction in leads with no first contact within the agreed window.

**Secondary (visibility):**
- Time for the owner to answer "what needs attention right now?" (from minutes/never → seconds).
- Unassigned high-value leads surfaced before they go cold.

**Business (the one that matters):**
- Bookings/viewings sourced through PAS.
- Partner's own assessment: "are fewer leads slipping than before?" — captured monthly.

**Qualitative:**
- Would the partner recommend PAS to another brokerage? (Track from week 2 onward.)

---

## 7. Term & graduation

- **Phase length:** ~60–90 days, reviewed at the monthly operational review.
- **Graduation:** when the success metrics are trending positive, convert to a standard relationship at protected founder pricing, and ask for the reference/intro.
- **Exit:** either side can end with a short notice; partner keeps their data, we keep the lessons. No lock-in — trust is the moat.

---

## 8. The ask (how to close it)

> "I'm taking on just a few design partners — brokerages I build this with, not sell to. You'd get founder pricing, direct influence, and a live view of your own pipeline, with zero risk because PAS only watches and answers; it never touches or acts on your data. In return I need real usage and honest feedback. Based on what we found in your audit, you're exactly the kind of operation I want to learn from. Want to be one of them?"
